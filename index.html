Running for Vice President
Candidate Statement
Candidate Statement — Vice President, USF ITE Student Chapter My name is Kwabena, and I am a first-year Master's student in Transportation Engineering at the University of South Florida. My academic focus encompasses traffic simulation, traffic flow theory, and transportation systems analysis, areas in which I have developed both technical competency and a genuine commitment to advancing the profession. I believe I am a strong candidate for Vice President because I bring hands-on experience with the tools and challenges that define modern transportation engineering. Through my graduate studies, I have worked extensively with industry-standard simulation software, engaged with applied traffic flow analysis, and contributed to projects with direct relevance to Florida's transportation agencies. These experiences have given me a grounded understanding of what transportation students are preparing for, and what a chapter like USF-ITE can do to close the gap between the classroom and the profession. As Vice President, I am committed to transforming USF-ITE into one of the most active and visible student organizations in the College of Engineering. That means moving beyond passive membership and building a chapter students genuinely want to be part of, through consistent programming, stronger industry connections, and a more visible presence on campus. I want to grow our membership by making the chapter relevant not just to those already committed to transportation engineering, but to any student curious about the field. Beyond engagement, I am committed to restoring USF-ITE's competitive legacy. Our chapter has a proven track record at the ITE Collegiate Traffic Bowl, having won the Florida Section District 10 championship and represented USF at the national level. That standard is one we can reach again, and I intend to prioritize structured preparation, study sessions, and team building to position USF-ITE to compete seriously at both the regional and national levels once more. I am running for this position because I believe a strong student chapter does more than host events. It shapes careers, builds professional identity, and creates a community that follows members long after graduation. I am prepared to bring the dedication and follow-through this role demands, and I would be honored to serve the USF-ITE community in this capacity.
